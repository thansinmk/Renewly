AI Usage

Artificial Intelligence plays two roles in sustainability. On one hand, the development and operation of AI systems, especially large-scale models, require significant computing power, leading to high energy consumption and carbon emissions. Training advanced AI models involves using powerful data centers that consume large amounts of electricity, often sourced from non-renewable energy, contributing to environmental impact. On the other hand, AI also plays a crucial role in promoting green technology and sustainability. It can optimize energy usage in smart grids, improve the efficiency of renewable energy systems, monitor environmental changes, and support sustainable agriculture and transportation. When used responsibly, AI can help industries reduce their carbon footprint and transition toward cleaner, more efficient technologies, balancing its environmental cost with significant benefits for a greener future.

Importance of AI

Prediction

In São Paulo, Brazil, a company called Sipremo is using AI to predict where and when climate disasters will occur, and what type of climate disasters they will be. The aim is to help businesses and governments better prepare for climate change and the growing challenges for communities that come with it. The company works in industries including insurance, energy, logistics and sport, where its analysis of disaster conditions and factors such as air quality can inform decisions on whether to delay or suspend events.

Reforesting

AI-powered computers are pairing up with drones in Brazil to reforest the hills around the coastal city of Rio de Janeiro. The computers define the targets and number of seeds to be dropped. A single drone can disperse 180 seed capsules per minute, which is 100 times faster than using human hands for traditional reforestation.

Impact of AI

Data Centers

Most large-scale AI deployments are housed in data centres, including those operated by cloud service providers. These data centres can take a heavy toll on the planet. The electronics they house rely on a staggering amount of raw materials. As well, the microchips that power AI need rare earth elements, which are often mined in environmentally destructive ways

Energy

Once a generative AI model is trained, the energy demands don’t disappear. Each time a model is used, perhaps by an individual asking ChatGPT to summarize an email, the computing hardware that performs those operations consumes energy. Researchers have estimated that a ChatGPT query consumes about five times more electricity than a simple web search.

Production

The production and disposal of AI hardware also contribute to growing concerns over electronic waste (e-waste) and resource depletion. AI relies on advanced computing hardware, which requires rare earth metals such as lithium and cobalt—minerals extracted through environmentally destructive mining processes. The production of a single AI chip requires more than 1,400 liters of water and 3,000 kWh of electricity.

Solutions

  • Use AI tools only when necessary (not for simple tasks).
  • Support AI companies that use clean energy or green data centers.
  • Encourage the development of smaller, more efficient AI models.
  • Reuse trained models instead of always building new ones.
  • Encourage companies to build “green” or eco-friendly AI.
